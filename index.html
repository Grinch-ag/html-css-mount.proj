TOP 5 DEADLIEST PEAKS IN THE WORLD

Ranking of the world's deadliest peaks

Okay, here we go. Let's start our rating with less dangerous peaks.

5. Kanchenjunga, 8586m

Mortality rate - 12.7%. Kanchenjunga, the third eight-thousandth mountain after Everest and K2, opens our rating. It is located in a remote area. The mountain has gained a very terrible fame just among women. For some reason it lets men in, while the opposite sex is mostly killed. There are only a few women who went up and came down alive, so for a long time the summit was impregnable just for the female half of humanity.

4. Dhaulagiri 8167 m

Mortality rate - 15.6%. It is noteworthy that despite its low altitude (the seventh peak of the world), Dhaulagiri is considered one of the most difficult eight-thousanders on the classic route. The reason for this is primarily avalanches, as well as falls and derailments, as the route runs along a very steep slope. The weather conditions for ascents are more favorable in spring and slightly worse in autumn.

3. Chogory (K2) 8611 m

Mortality rate - 19%. Often the summit of K2 (the world's second highest peak) tops the rankings among the most dangerous mountains. This is partly justified, as the mortality rate until recently was 42%. That is, practically every second person who went to the summit, did not return from it.

2. Nanga Parbat 8126 m

Mortality rate - 21%. Pakistani peak, the ninth eight-thousandth peak in the world. Some of the fatalities were not the fault of the mountain. The fact is that Pakistan often has a turbulent political environment. In 2013, part of the base camp was shot by the local Taliban. Unfortunately, many climbers died, including three Ukrainians. All of them are also included in the mountain's mortality rate. If we do not take into account the terrorist attack, the main causes of death on Nanga Parbat are also avalanches and falls from the wall. By the way, the Diamir Valley is considered one of the most beautiful places where the base camp is located. It is a large green area, above which rises the three-kilometer Diamir wall.

1. Annapurna I 8091 m

Mortality rate - 26,7%. The first place in the ranking of the world's deadliest peaks is occupied by Annapurna I. The mountain is one of the ten highest peaks in the world, but it is still the most dangerous. For all time on its peak only 266 people have been successfully visited - this is the smallest number of climbers among all eight-thousanders. The main cause of death is avalanches. But at the same time - it is the first eight-thousanders, which was climbed by a man. The first successful ascent was made by Maurice Herzog in 1950. An epic story that you can read a book about.
